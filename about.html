About ISRO

ISRO, India's space agency, was founded in 1969 to harness space technology for national development, space science, and exploration.

Highlights

  • Founded: 1969 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai
  • HQ: Bengaluru, India
  • Launch Vehicles: PSLV, GSLV, SSLV, LVM3
  • Missions: Chandrayaan, Mangalyaan, Aditya-L1

Leadership

ISRO operates under the Department of Space and is chaired by V. Narayanan, with national guidance from the Prime Minister of India.

Departments & Centres

  • VSSC - Launch systems
  • URSC - Satellite R&D
  • SAC - Communication payloads
  • ISTRAC - Tracking & Operations

Commercial & Industry Arms

  • NSIL - Commercial launches
  • Antrix - Marketing arm
  • IN-SPACe - Private industry access

Future Missions

  • Gaganyaan - Human spaceflight
  • Shukrayaan-1 - Venus mission
  • NISAR - Earth observation with NASA
  • RLV - Reusable Launch Vehicle

International Collaboration

ISRO collaborates with NASA, JAXA, ESA, CNES, Roscosmos, and others to enhance global scientific exchange and joint missions.
